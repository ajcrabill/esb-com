**Fall Community Progress Report — Year Two Communication**

October 24, 2028

---

Your board published its first community progress report in Fall 2027. That report answered one question: *What has your board accomplished in its first year of governance?* The answer was a list — board orientation, budget adoption, data reviews, community engagement, superintendent evaluation. It was the first time your board had told the full story of its governance work in one place.

Now it is Fall 2028. And the question is different.

The Fall 2028 community progress report answers: *What changed between Year One and Year Two of governance?*

That question cannot be answered with a list. It requires comparison. It requires the board to look at its own performance across two cycles and say: *Here is what was better this year. Here is what stayed the same. Here is what we need to improve.*

A community that sees its board comparing year-over-year performance is a community that trusts the board is serious about governance. Not because the report is perfect, but because the board is measuring itself — not just reporting.

This piece walks through the three sections every Year Two community progress report should contain: what your Fall 2027 report established, what changed in Year Two, and what the community should expect in Year Three.

---

### Part One: What the Fall 2027 Report Established

Your Fall 2027 community progress report was a first draft. It established three things that your board did not have before.

**First, it established that your board would report on its own performance.** Before Fall 2027, your community had never seen a school board voluntarily publish a governance progress report. Your board decided to be transparent about what it was doing, how it was governing, and whether student outcomes were improving. That decision alone separated your board from the vast majority of boards across the country — boards that govern behind closed doors and expect the community to trust them on faith.

**Second, it established a format.** Your board chose a structure — goals, data, decisions, next steps — and published it. The community learned what to expect. Journalists learned where to look for governance information. Staff learned that the board would communicate publicly about its work. The format mattered less than the consistency. A community that has seen one progress report from its board knows what a second report should look like.

**Third, it established a baseline.** The Fall 2027 report contained the first data points your board had ever published in a governance context. Third-grade reading proficiency at X percent. Eighth-grade math at Y percent. Chronic absenteeism at Z percent. Those numbers were not just information. They were the starting line. Every future report would measure against them.

The Fall 2027 report did not need to be perfect. It needed to exist. And it did.

Now your board has a year of governance data sitting next to that baseline. The Fall 2028 report is the first opportunity to show the community what a year of intentional governance produces.

---

### Part Two: What Changed in Year Two

This is the heart of the Fall 2028 report. The Year Two community progress report is not a repeat of Year One. It is a comparison across six dimensions of governance practice.

Here is what a six-dimension comparison looks like. Your board should publish this as a table in the community progress report. The table has three columns: the governance dimension, the Year One status, and the Year Two status. The table tells the Year Two story in two minutes.

| Governance Dimension | Fall 2027 | Fall 2028 |
|---|---|---|
| Data Reviews | First data review cycle completed. Board learned the three-decision protocol during the meeting. | Second data review cycle completed with year-over-year comparison baselines. Board arrived with decisions prepared. Meeting time reduced by one-third. |
| Budget Monitoring | Budget adopted under new framework. No quarterly monitoring cycle in place. | Budget monitored through three quarters. Goal-aligned expenditure review connected data to dollars in October. Structural variances flagged before January crosswalk. |
| Superintendent Evaluation | First evaluation completed under new framework. Board learned the process during execution. | Second evaluation completed with improved goal-aligned criteria. Board president facilitated the feedback process without committee detour. |
| Board Self-Evaluation | No formal board self-evaluation administered. | Board self-evaluation administered in Spring 2028. Results informed Summer Governance Renewal priorities. |
| Community Communication | First community progress report published. One-page format established. | Fall 2027 report format refined. Year-over-year comparison added. Community feedback channel established. |
| Summer Governance Work | No formal summer renewal process. | Protect-Apply-Add framework completed. Board named specific commitments to protect, apply more consistently, and add. Commitments tracked through fall execution cycle. |

That table is the Year Two story. Every row shows progress. Not perfection — there is no row that says "fully mastered." But every row shows a board that is further along in Year Two than it was in Year One. And that is the message the community needs to hear.

Here is what each row means for the community.

**Data Reviews.** Your board has now completed two full data review cycles. The first cycle was about learning the protocol. The second cycle was about applying the protocol with experience. The board's October 10 data review — covered in detail in [The October Data Review — Running Progress Monitoring With Experience](/october-data-review-experience-2028/) — ran faster, produced sharper decisions, and included year-over-year comparison data that the board did not have in Fall 2027. Your community should know that the board is not just looking at data. It is comparing this year's data against last year's data to answer the question: *Are students improving faster than they were last year?*

**Budget Monitoring.** In Fall 2027, your board adopted a budget under a new framework but had no quarterly monitoring system in place. This year, your board has monitored expenditures through three quarters of the fiscal year. The October 17 data-to-dollars connection — detailed in [From Data to Dollars](/data-to-dollars-october-2028/) — linked data review findings to expenditure patterns. The board flagged structural budget variances in October rather than discovering them in January. Your community should know that the board is watching how money connects to outcomes — not just approving a budget and walking away.

**Superintendent Evaluation.** The board completed its second superintendent evaluation this year with improved criteria tied directly to student outcome goals. The evaluation process was smoother because the board had done it before. The feedback was sharper because the board had a full year of data to reference. Your community should know that the board is holding the superintendent accountable against the goals the board adopted.

**Board Self-Evaluation.** This is a new dimension. In Year One, the board did not administer a formal self-evaluation. In Year Two, the board completed its first self-assessment and used the results to inform its Summer Governance Renewal. Your community should know that the board is measuring its own performance — not just the superintendent's.

**Community Communication.** The board published its first-ever community progress report in Fall 2027. That report established the standard. The Fall 2028 report adds year-over-year comparison data and a community feedback channel. Your community should know that the first report was not a one-time event. It is an annual practice.

**Summer Governance Work.** In Year One, the board entered the summer with no structured renewal process. In Year Two, the board completed a Protect-Apply-Add framework that named specific governance commitments for the fall cycle. Those commitments have been tracked through the October execution cycle. Your community should know that the board is not just governing year-to-year. It is renewing its practice intentionally.

Here is what your community will notice when they read this table. The board did not just continue doing what it did in Year One. It added new practices — board self-evaluation, summer renewal, year-over-year data comparison. It deepened existing practices — budget monitoring, superintendent evaluation, community communication. It is not the same board it was twelve months ago.

### Part Three: What the Community Should Expect in Year Three

Your board's Fall 2027 report answered: *What has the board accomplished?* Your Fall 2028 report answers: *What changed between Year One and Year Two?* Your Fall 2029 report will answer: *Is the board's governance producing better student outcomes?*

That progression — from accomplishment to improvement to outcome — is the arc of a maturing governance practice. Year Three is where cumulative work shows up in student outcome data.

Here is what your community should expect between now and Fall 2029.

**Year Three data reviews will be faster and more predictive.** By Fall 2029, your board will have three years of year-over-year comparison data. That means the board can identify trend lines, not just point-in-time measurements. The October 2029 data review will not ask *are third graders reading at grade level?* It will ask *are third graders reading at grade level faster than they were in each of the last two years?*

**Year Three budget monitoring will be fully integrated with data review.** By Fall 2029, the data-to-dollars connection will be a standing practice, not a new protocol. The board will review expenditure variance reports alongside goal trajectory data in the same meeting. The question will shift from *does the spending match the budget?* to *does the spending match the outcomes?*

**Year Three will include the board's second self-evaluation cycle.** The first self-evaluation was administered in Spring 2028. The second will be administered in Spring 2029. The comparison between the two will tell the board — and the community — whether the board's governance practice is improving in the areas the board itself identified.

**Year Three will produce the board's first three-year governance track record.** Three consecutive community progress reports. Three consecutive data review cycles. Three consecutive superintendent evaluations. Three consecutive budget cycles under the same framework. That track record is what earns a board long-term community trust — not a single good report, but a pattern of consistent governance.

Your board should name these expectations explicitly in the Fall 2028 community progress report. Not as promises. As a roadmap. The community does not need the board to guarantee specific outcomes. It needs the board to show that it knows where it is going.

### How to Produce the Fall 2028 Report

Your board has already produced most of the content for this report. The October 10 data review produced the goal trajectory comparison data. The October 17 data-to-dollars piece produced the budget monitoring narrative. The Summer Governance Renewal produced the Protect-Apply-Add commitments.

The Fall 2028 community progress report is an assembly of outputs your board has already generated — presented in a community-facing format. The board president or governance committee chair drafts it in a single working session that produces:
- The year-over-year goal comparison summary (from October data review outputs)
- The six-dimension governance comparison table (from the board calendar)
- The Summer Renewal commitment follow-through (from the October governance check)
- The Year Three priorities (from the board's annual roadmap)

One working session. One report. Published within one week of the data review.

### What Your Community Needs to Hear

Here is what your community needs to hear from your board this October, whether they know to ask for it or not:

*We have been doing this for two years. We are better than we were a year ago. We know where we need to improve. And we will keep reporting on all of it.*

That message — improvement, honesty, continuity — is what this report delivers. It is not a repeat of last year. It is the next chapter in a governance story your board is writing in public.

Your Fall 2027 report established that your board is willing to be transparent. Your Fall 2028 report establishes that your board is willing to measure whether it is getting better. That is the progress that matters — not just in student outcomes, but in governance itself.
