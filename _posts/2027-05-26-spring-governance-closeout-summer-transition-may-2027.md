**The Spring Governance Closeout — What Your Board Must Lock Before Summer**

May 26, 2027

---

The May arc is complete. Your board has done what most boards don't: run the Q3 data review with the year-to-date trend analysis and the resource-to-outcome reconciliation, built the evidence base for the superintendent evaluation, and started drafting the year-end governance report.

That is the spring governance cycle executed. Now the question is whether the board sustains the Focused Monitoring Cycle through June, July, and August — or whether summer governance drift undoes the work the board built across the spring.

I have seen the pattern more times than I can count. The board runs a disciplined spring cycle. The June board meeting is the last full meeting before summer. The board takes July off or shifts to a reduced calendar. The August meeting is a preview of the new school year. The September meeting is the first regular meeting — and the board discovers that no one has set the Q1 data review date for the fall, the committee structure has not been confirmed, and the board's monitoring calendar is empty for the first quarter of the new governance year.

The spring work is preserved by the summer structure. Here is what must be locked before the board leaves for summer.

**Lock one: the summer board work session calendar**

The board that takes the summer off from governance loses the continuity the spring cycle built. The board that runs one or two summer work sessions — not board meetings, work sessions — preserves the continuity and sets up the fall.

The summer work sessions should focus on three items that do not require a full board meeting agenda.

The first work session is the year-end governance report review. The draft exists. The board reviews it together, confirms the outcomes are accurately reflected, and authorizes publication. This work session should happen before June's regular board meeting or as a separate session in early June.

The second work session is the next-year goal framework. The board does not adopt new goals in summer — goal adoption happens in the fall after the new board members are seated and the data from the year-end report is available. But the board can discuss the framework: which goals from the current year should continue, which should be adjusted, and what new information the year-end report produced that should inform the next goal cycle.

The third work session is the board self-assessment and governance health planning. The board's governance health assessment from the mid-year check-in identified strengths and areas for growth. The summer work session is the time to plan the professional development, the committee assignments, and the governance protocol adjustments the board will implement in the fall.

The board that schedules these three work sessions before the summer break holds them. The board that waits until August to plan the summer loses the opportunity.

**Lock two: the July board retreat date**

The July board retreat is the most important governance event that happens outside a board meeting. It is the time when the board, away from the regular meeting agenda, does the strategic work that regular meetings do not accommodate.

The retreat should cover: the year-end governance report findings, the superintendent evaluation learnings, the board self-assessment results, the next-year goal framework, and the governance calendar for the first half of the next school year.

The board that schedules the retreat in April, confirms it in May, and locks it before summer break holds the retreat. The board that says "we should find a date in July" in June does not hold the retreat — or holds a poorly attended version because board members' summer schedules are already committed.

The retreat date must be confirmed before the summer break. The board chair or designated board member calls the retreat, confirms the date with all board members, and books the location. The board that enters summer with a confirmed retreat date enters summer with a governance calendar.

**Lock three: the Q1 data review date for the fall**

The Q1 data review for the next governance year — typically October or November — needs to be scheduled before the superintendent's fall calendar fills up. The superintendent needs to know the date to prepare the data structure, the data system integration, and the first-quarter data collection timeline.

The date should be set using the same protocol the board used for the Q3 review: a specific date, a confirmed format (thirty minutes, standard Focused Monitoring Cycle structure, three-outcome decision framework), and a confirmed preparation timeline (superintendent delivers data five calendar days before, board members receive data three calendar days before).

The board that sets the Q1 date in May has the date. The board that waits until September to set the Q1 date will find that the superintendent's calendar, the district assessment calendar, and the fall board meeting schedule have already consumed the available windows.

**Lock four: the committee structure for the first semester**

The board's committee structure — which committees exist, who chairs each committee, what each committee is charged to produce — should be confirmed before the summer break. Board members who know their summer committee assignments can do the preparatory work, schedule the summer meetings, and enter the fall with momentum.

The committee structure is not a summer activity. It is a late-May activity. The board that confirms assignments in May and communicates them in June has a committee structure that functions in August. The board that assigns committees in September has a committee structure that starts in October.

**The board that enters summer with a calendar**

The spring governance cycle — the Q3 data review, the superintendent evaluation, the year-end governance report, and the spring closeout — produces the evidence base for the governance year. The summer structure — the work sessions, the retreat, the Q1 data review date, the committee assignments — preserves the evidence base and converts it into action for the next year.

The board that enters summer with a calendar enters the fall with momentum. The board that enters summer with a wishlist enters the fall scrambling.

Your board built the spring infrastructure. Now lock the summer structure that preserves it.
