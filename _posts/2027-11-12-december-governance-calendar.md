**December Governance Calendar Planning — The Six-Week Sprint from November to Year-End**

November 12, 2027

---

The November board meeting is complete. The goal-drafting language is in progress. The community input process is open. The data review decisions are documented.

Now the board has six weeks until the calendar turns. Six weeks that include the annual organizational meeting, the year-end governance review, the final data review or progress check of the fall, and the January goal adoption preparation.

Here is what I have seen in boards that finish the year strong: they plan December in November. They do not wait for the December agenda to arrive and react to it. They build the December calendar intentionally, with every meeting and every governance action mapped to the year-end outcomes the board wants to produce.

**The six-week calendar**

From mid-November through the end of December, the board has five governance actions to schedule:

**Action one: The November data review follow-up (week of November 15).** The November data review or progress check should produce follow-up actions — directed changes that need monitoring, more information requested that needs delivery, and goal adjustments that need board discussion. The board should schedule a brief follow-up discussion at the next board meeting or as a separate agenda item.

**Action two: The goal language refinement session (week of November 29 or December 1).** By the end of November, the board should have draft goal language from the November goal-drafting work, plus community input from the input process. The board needs a dedicated session — sixty to ninety minutes — to review the community input, refine the goal language, and prepare the final draft for January adoption. This session should be scheduled before the December board meeting.

**Action three: The December annual organizational meeting (first or second week of December).** The annual organizational meeting sets the board's leadership structure for the coming year — board president, vice president, committee assignments, and meeting schedule. This meeting is also the board's opportunity to affirm the governance commitments the board made during the fall cycle and to set the governance calendar for January through June 2028.

**Action four: The year-end governance review and reset (second or third week of December).** The board should hold a dedicated review session — not a regular board meeting, a governance review — covering what the fall governance cycle produced, what the board accomplished against its goals, what the board learned, and what should carry forward into the 2028 governance year. This session produces the year-end governance report that the board publishes to the community.

**Action five: The superintendent evaluation cycle kickoff (third or fourth week of December).** The superintendent evaluation cycle for 2028 begins when the board communicates the evaluation framework, the timeline, and the evidence collection process to the superintendent. The December board meeting or a year-end work session is the right time to deliver this communication so the superintendent enters January knowing what the evaluation will measure.

**What the board president should do this week**

Three actions, before the November board meeting adjourns:

One. Draft the December governance calendar with the five actions listed above. Include dates, times, meeting formats (regular meeting, work session, executive session), and the board member or committee responsible for each action. Distribute the calendar to the full board before the November meeting adjourns.

Two. Confirm the December annual organizational meeting agenda with the board. The organizational meeting should include the board officer election, the committee assignment confirmations, the meeting schedule adoption for January through June, and a governance commitment affirmation. The board president should have a draft agenda ready for board review.

Three. Schedule the goal language refinement session. The board needs this session before the December board meeting. If the board does not have a dedicated work session slot, the board president should schedule a special meeting. The goal language must be refined before the January adoption vote, and December is the only window.

**What the board should expect from December**

December is the most compressed governance month of the year. The board has meetings, holidays, community events, and the natural end-of-year pressure on every professional's calendar.

Here is what I recommend boards accept: December is not the month to start new governance work. December is the month to close the work the board started in August, September, October, and November. Every governance action on the December calendar should be a completion action, not a launch action.

The board that plans December in November, schedules the completion actions in the first three weeks, and protects the fourth week for holiday recovery enters January with a completed fall cycle and a clear path to the 2028 goal adoption.
