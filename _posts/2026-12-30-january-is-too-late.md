I'll keep this one short. Two days until New Year's Eve. You have a window — right now — to do five things that will save your board the first two months of 2027.

Here's the truth: most boards don't meet between mid-December and mid-January. If your December meeting has come and gone, you won't sit together as a board again until after the holiday break. That's three to four weeks of governance downtime.

January arrives fast. The organizational meeting. The committee appointments. The first board meeting agenda. The goal-setting session that should have been scheduled in December. The superintendent evaluation that you meant to finalize before the break.

The boards that handle January well aren't the ones that work harder in January. They're the ones that acted in December. Here's what you can do this week — this exact week — to set your board up for a strong January.

**1. Approve your 2027 governance calendar before January 1.**

Send the proposed 2027 governance calendar to your board members by email. Regular meeting dates. Work session dates. The goal-setting session date (should be January or early February). The target dates for the board self-assessment, superintendent evaluation, and budget adoption. The community forum schedule.

Ask for approval via email or a brief virtual poll — whatever your board's governance policy allows. This is not a substitute for official board action, but it gives your board a framework for the year before you're in the room together. Formal adoption can happen at the January organizational meeting, but everyone arrives knowing what's on the calendar.

If your board doesn't have a 2027 governance calendar, this is the week to build one. Twelve months of regular meetings. Add the key deadlines: goal-setting work session, self-assessment window, superintendent evaluation, budget adoption, community forums. Block the dates now before the calendar fills with other things.

**2. Confirm your January goal-setting work session date and agenda.**

The single best thing you can do for 2027 is schedule — and I mean *schedule*, not just discuss — a dedicated goal-setting work session in January or early February. Not part of a regular board meeting. A separate work session, two to three hours, focused entirely on goal-setting.

This week, send a meeting invite to your board members and superintendent. Push for a date before January 20 if possible. The research is clear: boards that set goals in January are 2x more likely to report budget alignment with strategic priorities. Every week you wait after January 1 reduces that advantage.

**3. Finalize and deliver your superintendent evaluation.**

If your board's superintendent evaluation isn't complete yet, this is the week to close it. The board president should have the final document ready. Email it to the superintendent before January 1. Schedule the evaluation discussion for the first closed session in January — or if your board already delivered it, make sure the written evaluation is finalized and on record.

A superintendent who enters January knowing where they stand — what the board values, where the board sees gaps, what the board expects in the coming year — starts the year with clarity. A superintendent who waits until February to find out how the board sees their performance starts the year in limbo.

**4. Publish your year-end accountability snapshot.**

Monday's piece (keyword: Reflect) walked through the four-quadrant framework. If your board decided to publish an accountability snapshot, this week is the deadline. Before January 1, post it on the district website, include it in the community newsletter, or attach it to the January board meeting agenda.

The community deserves to see your board's honest accounting of the year before the new year begins. Not after. Before. It's a small act of governance transparency with outsized trust-building impact.

**5. Set your January board meeting agenda.**

This is the easiest action on the list and the one most boards overlook. The January agenda should have the governance reset work front-loaded:

- Organizational business: yes, it has to be there. Put it first.
- Goal-setting work session preview: include an agenda item that sets expectations for the separate work session scheduled in Action 2.
- Board self-assessment results review (if not completed in December): schedule 30 minutes.
- Superintendent evaluation discussion (if not completed): schedule closed session time.
- 2027 governance calendar review and formal adoption.
- Year-in-review acknowledgment: a brief public item where the board names the accomplishments and challenges of 2026.

A board that walks into its January meeting with this agenda already written — already distributed to members — starts the year in control of its calendar instead of reacting to it.
