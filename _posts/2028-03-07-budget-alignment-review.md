**Budget Alignment Review: Did February's Corrections Actually Happen?**

March 7, 2028

---

February was a heavy month. You built the crosswalk. You published the methodology. You set the budget adoption timeline. You requested specific corrections from the administration.

Now March asks one question: Did those corrections actually happen?

This is the alignment review. It is not a re-review of the full budget. It is a targeted check — three things, thirty minutes, one decision.

**Three things to check**

**1. Did corrections requested in February happen?**

You asked the administration to adjust specific line items or reallocate percentages in the crosswalk. Now you verify.

Pull the February meeting minutes. Read the exact language of the motion or direction. Then look at the revised budget document.

Did the shift happen? If yes, check the box and move on. If no, you have a problem — and you have exactly one month to solve it before the April public hearing.

Reference: Lawrence (KS) uses a simple two-page protocol for this. One page lists the requested corrections. The second page shows the administration's response. The board reviews both pages together in open session. Takes fifteen minutes.

**2. Does March Progress Monitoring data validate or challenge allocations?**

March is a Progress Monitoring month. You will receive data on each goal. That data does double duty.

First, it tells you whether students are on track.

Second, it tells you whether your budget allocations make sense. If a goal shows strong data, the allocation is validated. If a goal shows flat or declining data, the allocation needs a conversation.

Here is the question to ask: "Does the March data confirm that our budget is targeting the right things, or does it suggest we need to redirect resources before adoption?"

**3. Are fiscal guardrails still respected?**

Corrections change numbers. Every change must be checked against your board-adopted fiscal guardrails.

Three guardrails every board should have:
- Balanced budget (recurring revenues cover recurring expenses)
- Fund balance minimum (typically 15-20% of operating expenditures)
- Debt service coverage ratio

After February's corrections, recalculate each one. If guardrails hold, you are aligned. If a correction pushed a guardrail to the edge, flag it now — not at the hearing.

**If aligned, confirm and move to April**

The March alignment review ends with one of two outcomes:
- Aligned — confirm the budget, move to public hearing preparation
- Not aligned — one month to correct before the public hearing

No third option. April is the adoption. If February corrections did not land, March is the last window to fix them.

**What this means for the April hearing presentation**

If the alignment check passes, your April presentation writes itself: "In January, we set direction. In February, we built the crosswalk. In March, we verified alignment. Tonight, we present the aligned budget for adoption."

If it does not pass, the April presentation is different: "In January, we set direction. In February, we built the crosswalk. In March, we identified misalignment. We have spent the last month correcting it. Here is where we landed."

The public hearing audience deserves either story. Your job in March is to determine which one you will tell.
