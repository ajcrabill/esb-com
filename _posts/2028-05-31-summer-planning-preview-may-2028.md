**Summer Planning Preview — Setting Up the July-August Governance Arc**

2028-05-31

---

Your board has completed the May arc.

You have three data points for every adopted goal. You have a Budget Outcome finding for every allocation. You have an evaluation focus memo for the superintendent conversation. You have a Q2 Progress Report that consolidates everything into one document.

That is a governance achievement most boards never reach.

Now it is time to look forward.

The July-August governance arc is where the board transitions from the execution cycle to the planning cycle. The spring Accountability arc ran from March through May — evaluating alignment, reviewing data, projecting outcomes. The summer arc runs from July through August — refining goals, adjusting the governance framework, and preparing for the fall cycle.

This week's piece is the preview. It shows your board what the summer arc looks like, what the board should prepare between now and July, and how the work your board did in the spring sets up a productive summer.

---

**Why the summer arc matters**

Most boards treat June 30 as the finish line. The fiscal year ends. The board holds the superintendent evaluation. The board goes dark until the August board retreat.

I have watched this pattern for years. Boards that go dark for July and August lose the momentum they built in the spring. They arrive at the August retreat having forgotten the details of the Q2 Progress Report. They spend the retreat re-learning what they already knew in May. They start the fall cycle behind.

The summer arc prevents this.

The summer arc keeps the board engaged at the right level through July and August: not heavy operational meetings, but focused governance work that builds on the spring evidence. The board retreat. The goal refinement session. The governance calendar design. Each one takes the work from May and asks the forward-looking question.

Here is what the summer arc includes.

---

**The three summer priorities**

**Priority one: The board retreat.**

Every board should hold at least one governance retreat between July 1 and August 15. The retreat is not a social event. It is a working session with a specific agenda and specific outcomes.

Here is the agenda I recommend.

**Morning session: The year in review.**

The board reviews the Q2 Progress Report and asks: What did we learn from this governance cycle? Which practices produced better outcomes? Which practices need adjustment? The board does not re-litigate the spring data. The board asks what the spring data reveals about the board's own governance framework — its strengths, its gaps, and its opportunities for improvement.

I have watched boards in **Blue Valley (KS)** structure this session effectively. Their board president distributes the Q2 Progress Report two weeks before the retreat. Board members arrive having read it. The first ninety minutes of the retreat are not spent reviewing the report — they are spent discussing the board's assessment of its own performance. The question is not "Where did the students land?" but "How did our governance framework contribute to where the students landed?"

**Afternoon session: Goal refinement for H2.**

The board reviews the goals adopted in January and asks: Which goals should we continue into the second half of the year, which should be revised based on what we learned, and which should be retired with a written finding documenting the learning?

This is not a new goal-setting process. The board does not re-set the goals from scratch. The board refines the existing goals based on the spring evidence. A goal that is on track and sustainable: the board confirms it for the second half of the year. A goal that is off track and the board understands why: the board revises the goal or the strategy based on the evidence. A goal that is off track and the board does not fully understand why: the board keeps the goal and directs a diagnostic study to inform the fall goal-setting cycle.

**Priority two: The Board Self-Evaluation.**

The summer arc includes the Board Self-Evaluation — the board's formal assessment of its own governance performance. This is a June piece in the full arc overview, and it connects directly to the July-August arc.

The Board Self-Evaluation asks the same question the Q1 Governance Health Check asked in March, but at the full-year level: Did our governance framework produce better outcomes than we would have achieved without it?

The evidence is the full cycle: the January goals, the February crosswalk, the March alignment check and health check, the April adoption, the May data and outcome reviews, and the June evaluation. The board reviews the evidence and answers one question per domain of the adopted governance framework: Did this practice contribute to student outcomes, or did it add process without impact?

Boards that answer this question honestly often discover that two or three of their governance practices drove the majority of their improvement. They also discover one or two practices that consumed meeting time without producing measurable change. The Board Self-Evaluation tells them which is which.

**Priority three: The July-August governance calendar.**

The board designs the governance calendar for July and August. Not the operational calendar — the board does not need to approve a meeting schedule for the custodial staff. The governance calendar: the board retreat date, the goal refinement session date, the Board Self-Evaluation session date, any special board calls, and the preparation timeline for each session.

Here is the recommended calendar:

- **Mid-July:** Board retreat (year in review + goal refinement)
- **Late July:** Board Self-Evaluation session
- **Early August:** Governance calendar design for the fall cycle
- **Mid-August:** July and August governance closeout

The calendar is light — two to three meetings over eight weeks — but each meeting has a specific governance outcome. No filler. No standing agendas that run out of topics. Each meeting produces a written finding or a decision that moves the board's governance work forward.

---

**What the board should do between now and July**

The Summer Planning Preview is not a call to action for June. Your board has the superintendent evaluation and the year-end closeout to complete in June. The summer work happens in July and August.

But there are two things the board should do before the July retreat to set up a productive summer.

**One: Distribute the Q2 Progress Report to all board members with a reading deadline of June 25.**

This ensures every board member arrives at the retreat having reviewed the full year's evidence. The board president can add one question to the reading assignment: "As you read the Q2 Progress Report, note three things the board did well this year and one thing the board should do differently next year."

**Two: Distribute the Board Self-Evaluation instrument to all board members by July 1.**

The Board Self-Evaluation instrument should have been adopted by the board as part of the governance framework earlier in the year. If your board has not adopted a formal self-evaluation instrument, the June superintendent evaluation arc includes guidance on selecting one. Distribute it in early July so board members have time to complete it thoughtfully before the late July session.
