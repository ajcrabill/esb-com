**Final Budget Adoption: What Your Board's Vote Actually Commits To**

April 18, 2028

---

The moment arrives. The superintendent presents the final budget. The board chair calls for a motion. You raise your hand. The budget is adopted.

What did you just vote on?

If you think the answer is "a spending plan," you are underselling it. The adoption vote commits your board to four distinct things.

**What you vote on: Four commitments**

**1. The Budget Document.** Yes, this is the spreadsheet. Revenues. Expenditures. Fund balances. The numbers have to add up. But if this is the only thing you are evaluating, your board is operating at the compliance level.

**2. Goal Alignment Finding.** This is the finding you developed through the crosswalk, refined in the March review, and finalized after the public hearing. It states: the proposed budget allocates resources sufficient to make progress on each adopted goal.

**3. Fiscal Guardrail Finding.** This is your board's statement that the budget operates within every fiscal guardrail — fund balance minimums, debt service limits, every constraint the board set. The finding names each guardrail and confirms compliance.

**4. Community Alignment Finding.** This is where the engagement loop closes. The finding states whether the budget reflects what the community asked for — from fall listening sessions through the public hearing.

**What changes after adoption**

Adoption is a transition, not a termination.

| Dimension | Before Adoption | After Adoption |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Board focus | Budget development | Implementation monitoring |
| Superintendent focus | Budget building | Budget execution |
| Primary tool | Goal-to-budget crosswalk | Progress Monitoring dashboard |
| Board role | Evaluator of the plan | Partner in execution |

Notice what stays the same: your goals. They were the destination in January. They were the criteria in February, March, and April. They remain the measure through December.

**The timeline at a glance**

| Month | Milestone |
|-------|-----------|
| January | Board adopts goals |
| February | Goal-to-budget crosswalk |
| March | Budget alignment review |
| April (early) | Public hearing + debrief |
| April (late) | Budget adopted with three findings |

Four months from goals to adoption. That is the timeline of a board that governs on purpose.

A board that adopted the budget with all three findings has evidence that the budget funds its goals. A board that adopted without findings has no evidence — and will spend the rest of the fiscal year wondering whether the budget and the goals are aligned.
