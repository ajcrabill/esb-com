**The August Board Work Session — The Single Meeting That Closes Summer and Opens Fall**

August 16, 2027

---

The board has been working all summer. The superintendent check-in. The training agenda. The community progress report. The goal-setting foundation work. The reading. The policy audit. The governance team discipline check. The data review system design. The budget cycle preparation. The fall readiness audit.

Ten pieces of governance work across six weeks. Some completed. Some in progress. Some deferred.

Now it is mid-August. The first September board meeting is three weeks away. And there is one meeting that determines whether the summer work produces a September running start or a September scramble: the August board work session.

**Why the August work session is different from every other board meeting**

The August board work session is not a regular board meeting. It is not a committee meeting. It is a dedicated work session with a single purpose: to review every piece of summer work, decide what is complete and what needs more work, and produce a single document — the September launch agenda — that tells every board member, the superintendent, and the community exactly what the board is doing in September.

The August work session is the meeting where the board closes the summer governance cycle and opens the fall governance cycle. It should be treated with the same seriousness as the January goal-setting meeting, the June evaluation meeting, and the April budget adoption meeting.

**The work session agenda**

The August board work session should have six agenda items. Each item covers a piece of summer work and produces a specific outcome.

**Item one: Summer work closeout review (25 minutes)**

The board reviews the fall readiness audit completed in the last week of July. For each item on the audit, the board confirms: is this item ready, in progress with a clear August completion plan, or deferred to the fall?

The outcome of this item: a completed summer work closeout summary — one page listing every item, its status, and the next action for items that are not yet complete.

This summary becomes the board's reference document for the first September board meeting. When a board member asks, "What did we accomplish this summer?" the board president points to the closeout summary.

**Item two: Superintendent check-in follow-up confirmation (15 minutes)**

The board reviews the July check-in follow-up (covered in the Aug 6 piece). The check-in documentation is shared. The board support delivery assessment is reviewed. The updated growth areas and support commitments for the fall are confirmed.

The outcome of this item: a confirmed, written superintendent partnership agreement for the fall — covering the growth areas, the board's support commitments, and the check-in schedule for the fall (October evaluation checkpoint, January mid-year evaluation).

**Item three: Budget guidance document first reading (30 minutes)**

The board reviews the draft budget guidance document prepared by the board president or finance chair (covered in the Aug 13 piece). Each section is read and discussed: strategic priorities, resource allocation principles, budget development parameters, timeline and decision points, and linkage to goal outcomes.

The outcome of this item: a revised draft with the board's feedback incorporated, ready for the board president to share with the superintendent for informal feedback before the October formal conversation.

**Item four: Fall governance calendar finalization (20 minutes)**

The board reviews the fall governance calendar — drafted in June, refined through July — and confirms that every item is scheduled. The calendar should include: the September board meeting (agenda confirmed), the October data review and budget guidance conversation, the November board meeting, the December goal-setting preview and policy adoption reading, the January goal-setting adoption and mid-year evaluation, the February data review, the March budget review, the April budget adoption, and the May board meeting.

The outcome of this item: a finalized fall governance calendar, distributed to every board member and the superintendent, and published on the district website.

**Item five: September board meeting agenda preparation (20 minutes)**

The board drafts the September board meeting agenda. The first fall board meeting should include: the community progress report feedback response (from the Aug 9 piece), the policy discussion and first reading (covered in the existing Aug 18 piece), the budget guidance document informal superintendent feedback, the fall governance calendar confirmation, and the board's goal-setting preview for the upcoming year.

The outcome of this item: a draft September board meeting agenda with specific agenda items, estimated time allocations, and assigned responsibilities.

**Item six: Summer governance arc closeout — lessons learned (10 minutes)**

The board reflects on the summer governance arc. Questions to discuss: what worked about the board's summer governance approach? What did not work? What would the board do differently for next summer's governance arc?

The outcome of this item: three to five lessons learned that the board will apply to the next summer governance cycle. These lessons become part of the board's governance practice documentation.

**How to schedule the work session**

The August board work session should be scheduled in the third week of August — not earlier and not later. Earlier than mid-August and too much of the summer work is still in progress. Later than mid-August and the September board meeting preparation becomes rushed.

The work session should be three hours. No shorter. The six agenda items require at least two hours of discussion time, and the remaining hour accounts for transitions, breaks, and the inevitable items that require more discussion than planned.

The work session should be in person. Virtual work sessions are better than no work session, but the August work session requires the kind of board-to-board conversation that is harder to facilitate remotely. If the board cannot meet in person, schedule two virtual sessions of ninety minutes each rather than one three-hour virtual session.

**What the board walks away with**

At the end of the August board work session, the board should have four documents:

1. The summer work closeout summary — what was accomplished, what is still in progress, what was deferred
2. The fall superintendent partnership agreement — the growth areas, support commitments, and check-in schedule
3. The draft budget guidance document — ready for superintendent feedback
4. The finalized fall governance calendar — with the September board meeting agenda drafted

Four documents. Three hours. One meeting that closes summer and opens fall.

The board that holds the August work session enters September with clarity, alignment, and a plan. The board that does not hold the work session enters September with the summer work scattered across individual board members' notes, inboxes, and memories — and the first September board meeting is spent trying to reconstruct what the board planned to do.
