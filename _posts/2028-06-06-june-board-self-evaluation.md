**June Board Self-Evaluation: How Did You Perform in the First Half of the 2028 Governance Cycle?**

June 6, 2028

---

When does your board evaluate itself?

If you said "once a year in December," you are selling yourself short.

The board self-evaluation is not an annual event. The best boards evaluate themselves at least twice a year. June is the natural mid-cycle checkpoint. You are six months into the 2028 governance cycle. You have adopted goals. You have passed a budget. You have conducted progress monitoring. You have held public hearings. Now is the time to ask: *How did we do?*

Not the superintendent. Not the staff. You. The board.

**The Five Dimensions**

Your September 2027 self-evaluation established a baseline. You scored your board across five Governance Team dimensions. Those same five dimensions are the right framework for your June 2028 checkpoint.

*One.* Goal Clarity. Did you adopt measurable goals this cycle? Did those goals actually drive your decisions, or did they sit in a binder? When the board made a choice about time, money, or priorities, did you ask "Does this serve our goals?" before you voted?

*Two.* Progress Monitoring. Did you check data monthly? Did you receive progress reports on every adopted goal? Did you make decisions from that data, or did you just receive it and move to the next agenda item?

*Three.* Budget Alignment. Did you connect spending to goals? When you adopted the 2028-2029 budget in April, could you trace every material expenditure back to a specific student outcome goal? If a board member asked "What goal does this line item serve?" at the dais, could your superintendent answer?

*Four.* Superintendent Partnership. Did you provide clear direction and support? Not micromanagement. Not abdication. Direction and support. Did your board speak with one voice after every meeting? Did your superintendent know what you expected between meetings?

*Five.* Board Operations. Did your committees and meetings produce what the governance calendar called for? Did the board's meeting time go to strategic discussion or operational weeds? Did committee work advance board priorities or stall?

**Baseline vs. Checkpoint**

The September 2027 self-evaluation gave you a baseline score across each dimension. Your June 2028 self-evaluation gives you a mid-cycle comparison. Where have you improved? Where have you stayed the same? Where have you regressed?

This is not a pass-fail exercise. This is a calibration. You are comparing the board that started the cycle against the board that has been governing for six months. The question is not "Are we perfect?" The question is "Are we better than we were?"

**One to Two Commitments**

The self-evaluation must produce specific, measurable commitments for the second half of the governance cycle. Not "We will communicate better." That is a wish, not a commitment.

A real commitment sounds like this: "The board will adopt a consent agenda policy to reduce full-board meeting time from 90 minutes to 60 minutes, effective the July work session." Or: "The board will request a five-minute progress monitoring update on Goal One at every regular meeting through December."

One commitment. Two at most. Measurable. Enforceable. Written down.

**The 45-Minute Conversation**

The self-evaluation is not a homework assignment. It is a board conversation. The board president leads it. It takes 45 minutes. It produces written findings.

Here is the structure: President reads each dimension aloud. Each board member shares their score and a one-sentence rationale. The board discusses the range of scores. The board identifies the gap between baseline and checkpoint. The board agrees on one or two H2 improvement commitments. The secretary records everything.

Forty-five minutes. Written findings. No excuses.

**What You Walk Away With**

When you finish this self-evaluation, you have three things you did not have when you started.

First: a clear picture of where your board stands at mid-cycle.
Second: a documented comparison between September 2027 and June 2028.
Third: a short, specific list of governance practices you will improve in the second half of the cycle.

That is what a governing board does. It checks its own work. It does not wait for December to find out.
