**Implementation Mode: What Changes After the Budget Is Adopted**

April 25, 2028

---

The vote is over. The budget is adopted. The applause dies down.

Now what?

Most boards drift through the next four months. They review last-minute budget adjustments. They approve purchase orders. They spend May through September acting as an administrative review committee rather than a governance board.

That drift is a choice. And it's costing your students.

**The board's job has changed. Fundamentally.**

Before adoption, your primary governance tool was the crosswalk. You asked: *Does this budget connect our goals to our allocations?*

After adoption, your primary governance tool is the Progress Monitoring dashboard. You now ask: *Are the funded initiatives producing results?*

Same goals. Same allocations. Different question entirely.

**Three Things That Change Immediately**

**1. Meeting agendas — from budget review to Progress Monitoring**

Before adoption, your agenda committees spent their time reviewing budget drafts, line-item comparisons, and alignment tables.

After adoption, every agenda should include a Progress Monitoring check. Not a report. A check. The superintendent presents data on the adopted goals. The board asks questions about trajectory, not about line items.

Review last week's agenda and next week's agenda side by side. If they look the same, you haven't made the transition.

**2. Superintendent reporting — from budget drafts to implementation updates**

Before adoption, the superintendent's reports to the board were about what the budget would look like.

After adoption, the superintendent's reports shift to implementation: What has been executed? What data do we have? What is the trajectory toward each goal?

If the superintendent is still presenting budget spreadsheets in May, that's a signal the board hasn't communicated the transition. You need to say it explicitly: *The budget is adopted. We now want implementation updates.*

**3. Community conversations — from 'here's what we plan to spend' to 'here's what we're getting for what we're spending'**

Before adoption, you went to the community and said: *Here's our plan. Here's how we'll spend your tax dollars.*

After adoption, you go to the community and say: *Here's what we're getting for what we're spending. Here's the progress toward the goals we all agreed on.*

The community doesn't care about line items. They care about outcomes. Give them outcomes.

**The Governance Calendar Reset**

Your board created a Q1 governance calendar back in January. It covered the budget adoption arc — goal setting, crosswalk development, public hearing planning, adoption.

That calendar expires this week.

You need a Q2 governance calendar. Right now. Covering every meeting through June.

Ask yourself: *What does every meeting through June need to produce now that the budget is adopted?*

Not what will be reviewed. What will be *produced*. A decision. A direction. A confirmation. A correction.

Schedule it now or you'll fill the time with administrative noise.

**The Trap Most Boards Fall Into**

Here's what happens when a board doesn't make this transition:

The board spends May reviewing budget amendments. The superintendent brings forward small adjustments. The board debates them. The next meeting, more adjustments. By June, the board hasn't looked at Progress Monitoring data once.

Then July arrives. The new fiscal year starts. And the board has no idea whether the first two months of implementation are on track.

By August, the board is behind. By September, they're reacting instead of governing.

Don't let that be your board.

**The Transition Is Simple. The Discipline Is Not.**

The concept is straightforward: shift from crosswalk to Progress Monitoring. The discipline is in the execution — scheduling it, enforcing it, refusing to backslide into budget review mode.

Your board made a commitment in January. You adopted goals. You built a crosswalk. You held a public hearing. You voted on findings.

Now you owe it to the community to follow through.

Implementation mode starts now.
