**Setting the Fall Superintendent Partnership Rhythm — The Difference Between Alignment and Drift**

September 3, 2027

---

The September board meeting launched the fall governance cycle. The board reviewed goal progress, confirmed the October data review logistics, and distributed the fall governance calendar.

Now comes the relationship work that determines whether the fall governance cycle runs on alignment or drift.

Every board president I work with tells me the same thing in November: "I thought we were on the same page about the fall priorities, and then something came up that I didn't expect." The "something" is almost never a crisis. It is almost always a communication gap that started in September and grew through October.

The summer check-in covered growth areas and support commitments. The September board meeting confirmed the governance direction. What is missing in most boards is the operating rhythm — the regular cadence of check-ins that keeps the board president and superintendent aligned between board meetings.

Here is the rhythm every board should set in the first week of September.

**The biweekly check-in**

I recommend a standing thirty-minute check-in every two weeks between the board president and the superintendent. No agenda required beyond three questions:

1. What progress have we made against the fall governance priorities since our last check-in?
2. What is emerging that the board needs to know about — positive or concerning?
3. What does the board president need to do in the next two weeks to support the superintendent?

That is the entire agenda. It is not a governance meeting. It is a communication meeting. The board president is not directing the superintendent. The board president is staying informed so the board's governance decisions remain grounded in current reality.

**The written check-in memo**

Every other check-in — every four weeks — should produce a brief written memo from the board president to the full board. One page. Three sections: what we discussed, what is emerging, and what the board needs to do or be aware of before the next board meeting.

The check-in memo solves a specific problem I see in nearly every board I coach: the board president knows what is happening because the president talks to the superintendent regularly. The rest of the board does not. And by the time the full board finds out what the president already knows, the board is reacting rather than governing.

The check-in memo is not confidential. It is a governance communication tool. Every board member should receive it within 48 hours of the check-in meeting.

**The pre-board-meeting calibration**

Before every board meeting, the board president and superintendent should have a fifteen-minute calibration conversation. The purpose is not to plan the meeting — the agenda is already set. The purpose is to identify any emerging dynamics that could affect the meeting. A community concern that has surfaced. A board member who has raised a question outside the meeting. A staff development that could shift the board's perception of progress.

The calibration prevents the board meeting surprise — the moment when the superintendent presents information that the board president did not know was coming, and the board reacts without preparation.

**The post-board-meeting debrief**

After every board meeting, the board president and superintendent should have a ten-minute debrief. Three questions:

1. What went well in the meeting from a governance perspective?
2. What could have gone better from a governance perspective?
3. Is there anything that emerged in the meeting that changes the fall calendar or priorities?

The debrief is the board president's most important learning tool. It is also the superintendent's most important feedback mechanism. Every board-superintendent partnership I have seen break down could have been preserved by a consistent post-meeting debrief that caught the small issues before they became big issues.

**The September commitment**

Here is what I recommend every board president do this week: schedule the biweekly check-ins through December. Send the superintendent a calendar invitation for every other Wednesday at 10:00 AM or whatever cadence works. Set the expectation that the check-in happens even when there is nothing urgent to discuss — because the check-in is not about urgency. It is about alignment.

The board president who maintains a consistent partnership rhythm through the fall enters December with a superintendent who trusts the board, a board that trusts the president, and a governance cycle that ran on alignment rather than drift.
