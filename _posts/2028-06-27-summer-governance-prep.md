**Summer Governance Prep: Your Reading List and Action Items Before Fall**

June 27, 2028

---

The board's formal meeting schedule pauses for summer. But governance work continues.

Here is the distinction every board member needs to understand. The board's *calendar* goes quiet. The board's *work* does not.

June is the spring arc's closing chapter. Your board has conducted its self-evaluation. You have completed end-of-year Progress Monitoring. You have previewed the fall calendar. Now it is time to prepare yourself for the second half of the governance cycle.

**Five Summer Action Items**

You have five things to do between now and the first meeting in September.

*One.* Review the H1 board self-evaluation findings. Read the written report. Identify the one or two improvement commitments your board made. Think about what you personally need to do differently to support those commitments.

*Two.* Read the end-of-year Progress Monitoring report. Know where each goal stands. Know which goals are on track and which need accelerated attention. You cannot contribute to a productive summer work session if you have not read the data.

*Three.* Review the H2 governance calendar your board adopted in June. Understand what is coming in July, August, September, October, November, and December. Mark your personal calendar with the key dates.

*Four.* Complete any board member training required by your state. Summer is the window. Do not wait until the week before the September meeting. Do it now while you have space to think.

*Five.* Prepare questions for the summer work session. What do you need clarified about the June data? What do you want to understand about the goals needing accelerated attention? What questions will help the board make better decisions in the fall?

Five actions. None of them require a board meeting. All of them require your attention.

**Recommended Reading**

Summer is the time to build your governance knowledge. Here is what I recommend.

*The Essential School Board Member* — my book. Specifically the chapters on goal-setting cycles and board self-evaluation. Read them alongside your board's actual self-evaluation findings. You will see your own board reflected in the framework, and you will find specific practices to improve.

*The Crosswalk Methodology* piece from February 2028. If you were on the board when this piece was published, read it again. The crosswalk between goals and budget is not a one-time document. Reading the methodology a second time reveals things you missed the first time when you were in the middle of the budget adoption process.

*The Q1 Governance Health Check results.* Review the first-quarter data from your district's health check. Compare it against the end-of-year data. What changed? What did not?

Reading is not optional for governing board members. You cannot make informed decisions without informed thinking. Summer gives you the time to do that thinking.

**The Summer Work Session**

Your summer work session — planned in the May 30 piece and confirmed in your H2 calendar — should happen in July or early August. Before the back-to-school crunch.

What does the work session cover? Three things.

First, a deep review of the June Progress Monitoring data. The board should have a conversation about each goal, not a presentation from the superintendent. Board members should come with questions and leave with understanding.

Second, a discussion of the 2029 goal-setting timeline. When will drafts be delivered? When will the board discuss them? When will the community be invited to respond?

Third, the board's H2 improvement commitments. The self-evaluation produced them. The work session is where the board ensures they are actionable.

If your summer work session is not scheduled yet, schedule it now. Do not let August arrive without a date on the calendar.

**The September Trade-Off**

Here is what I have observed across hundreds of boards.

Boards that invest summer time in governance preparation enter September having already done the thinking. Their first fall meeting is strategic. They are ready to receive data, make decisions, and move forward.

Boards that do not invest summer time enter September reacting to whatever arrives first. Their first fall meeting is catching up. They spend September asking basic questions they could have answered in July.

You have a choice. Which kind of board are you?

**Celebrate the Execution**

Summer is also the moment to recognize what your board accomplished January through June.

Think back to January. You adopted measurable goals. You began Progress Monitoring. You completed the goal-to-budget crosswalk. You held a public hearing. You adopted the budget. You evaluated yourselves at mid-cycle. You conducted end-of-year Progress Monitoring. You adopted the H2 governance calendar.

That is a lot of governance execution in six months.

Boards that govern on purpose should recognize their own execution. Not for ego. For momentum. When you see what a focused governance cycle produces, you are more likely to do it again.

Take a moment to acknowledge your board's work. Then use summer preparation to make the second half even stronger.
