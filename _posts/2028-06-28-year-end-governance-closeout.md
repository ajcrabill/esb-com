**Year-End Governance Closeout — Academic Year Wrap-Up and Summer Preparation**

June 28, 2028

---

The superintendent evaluation is complete. The board self-evaluation is complete. The spring governance cycle is closing.

Last week's piece walked you through the board self-evaluation protocol — the four questions that assess whether the board's governance produced better decisions than it would have made without them. This week closes the cycle.

This is the last newsletter of the 2027-28 academic year. It is also the most important one for a specific reason: it is where the board names what it accomplished, what it learned, and what it will do differently in the fall.

Here is what I have learned about year-end closeouts from watching boards across the country.

Most boards end the year the same way they begin it — with no written record of what the governance cycle produced. The board reviews a year-end data presentation, thanks the superintendent and staff for their work, and adjourns for the summer. Three months later, the board reconvenes in August and begins planning the fall cycle from scratch, relying on memory and individual recollections of what worked and what did not.

A year-end governance closeout is the board's opportunity to capture the governance cycle's output in a single document that the fall board can open and use as the starting point for the next cycle.

Let me show you what the closeout document contains.

---

**Section one: The governance cycle accomplishments**

The board names its top accomplishments from the 2027-28 governance year. These are not operational requirements like approving the budget on time. These are governance accomplishments that would not have occurred without the board's framework.

"The board adopted three specific, measurable student outcome goals from baseline data in January 2028 and conducted three Progress Monitoring check-ins during the spring cycle — the first time the board has measured progress toward goals between adoption and year-end."

"The board conducted a budget crosswalk in February 2028 that identified two misalignments between goal priorities and resource allocation. The misalignments were corrected in the adopted April budget."

"The board conducted a Q1 Governance Health Check in March 2028 and a full board self-evaluation in June 2028 — the first time the board has assessed whether its own governance framework produced better decisions than it would have made without it."

"The board completed a superintendent evaluation in June 2028 using a rubric aligned to the adopted goals, with individual written findings submitted by each board member before the deliberation meeting, a structured deliberation conversation, and a written finding presented in open session."

---

**Section two: The governance gaps identified**

The board names the gaps the cycle revealed. These are the findings from the Q1 Health Check, the board self-evaluation, and the superintendent evaluation that the board committed to addressing.

"Calendar execution: The board executed 74 percent of the planned governance actions through May. Three of the five missed actions were Progress Monitoring conversations that were replaced by operational agenda items. The board commits to designating Progress Monitoring as a non-deferrable governance action in the fall governance calendar."

"Community engagement: The board conducted the April public hearing as required by law but cannot identify a specific budget allocation that changed as a result of community input. The board commits to conducting a community input session before the fall budget direction is written, with the explicit purpose of surfacing community priorities before the board's direction is set."

"Governance team functioning: The board identified during the self-evaluation that two board members consistently voted against budget items without providing alternatives during discussion. Both members committed to presenting alternative proposals during fall budget conversations."

---

**Section three: The summer governance actions**

The board names the governance work that happens between June and August.

Summer is not a governance off-season — it is a preparation season. Three summer actions determine whether the fall cycle launches with clarity or with drift.

**Summer action one: Board retreat or work session planning.** The board schedules its summer retreat or work session. The retreat is where the board reviews the year-end governance closeout document, names any adjustments to the governance framework for the fall, and begins the fall calendar planning process. I recommend the retreat occur no later than the first week of August.

**Summer action two: Goal refinement for H2 of the calendar year.** The board reviews the adopted goals from January. Most should continue into the fall with the same targets. Some may need adjustment based on summer data releases. A goal that was met may need a higher target. A goal where the trajectory is off track may need a revised strategy — confirm the superintendent has a plan to present when the board returns.

**Summer action three: Fall governance calendar development.** The board president or governance committee drafts the fall governance calendar, naming what each board meeting from August through December is designed to produce. It mirrors the spring cycle in structure — goal confirmation, Progress Monitoring, budget alignment review — with adjustments identified in the year-end closeout.

I recommend the board president circulate the draft fall calendar by August 1. Board members arrive at the August retreat ready to discuss, not ready to draft. A board that drafts the calendar during the retreat loses a third of the time to logistics. A board that reviews a draft uses the time for strategic discussion.

---

**Section four: The fall launch preview**

Here is the template language for the fall preview, even before specific dates are confirmed:

"The board will reconvene in August for a governance retreat to review the year-end closeout document, refine adopted goals, and confirm the fall governance calendar. The September through December governance cycle will include:

— Goal confirmation and Progress Monitoring baseline update (September)
— Fall data review with year-over-year comparison (October)
— Budget alignment review for the mid-year budget revision (November)
— Q4 Governance Health Check and mid-year superintendent check-in (December)

"The fall cycle incorporates the adjustments identified in the June board self-evaluation, including a calendar with non-deferrable Progress Monitoring actions, a community input session before the fall budget direction is written, and a board discussion protocol that requires minority perspectives to be engaged before votes."

A board that publishes this preview in June tells the community and superintendent that governance work continues through summer and relaunches in the fall with adjustments based on what the board learned.

---

**What the board delivers to the fall board**

The year-end closeout document is a transfer of governance knowledge from the spring board to the fall board. Even if every board member returns in August — and many boards gain new members after the June elections — the closeout document ensures the fall board starts from the spring board's assessment, not from scratch. A new board member who reads it in August enters the first meeting with context that would otherwise take months to develop through meeting attendance alone.

I tell every board I coach: your most important governance document is not the policy manual, the budget book, or the strategic plan. It is the year-end closeout document that transfers the governance cycle's assessment from one year to the next. A board that writes that document every June builds a cumulative record of governance improvement that compounds year over year.
