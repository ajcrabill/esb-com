**Post-Hearing Board Actions: Finalizing Findings Before the Adoption Vote**

April 11, 2028

---

The public hearing is over. You listened. You took notes. You heard from parents, teachers, and community members.

Now what?

The hearing is not the finish line. It is the last checkpoint before the adoption vote. Between the hearing and that vote, your board has three specific actions to take.

**Action 1: Debrief the hearing**

Schedule a 30-minute board debrief within 48 hours of the hearing. Not a full meeting — a working session. Go around the table: "What did you hear that surprised you?"

The answers tell you whether community input validated your direction or revealed a gap. Write down the themes. They go into your hearing summary memo.

**Action 2: Finalize the three findings**

Your board has been developing three findings since February:

1. Goal alignment finding — Does the proposed budget advance each adopted goal?
2. Fiscal guardrail finding — Does the budget operate within every named guardrail?
3. Community alignment finding — Does the budget reflect what the community asked for?

Now incorporate hearing feedback. Did a parent's testimony reveal a monitoring gap in Goal 3? Did a teacher's question surface ambiguity in Guardrail 2? Update the findings accordingly.

Without findings, you are voting on a spreadsheet. With findings, you are voting on a governance decision.

**Action 3: Confirm last-minute budget adjustments**

The superintendent may have heard something at the hearing that warrants a last adjustment. Confirm those adjustments in writing before the adoption vote. Any change should be documented, explained, and shared with the full board at least 72 hours before the vote.

No surprises on adoption night.

**The hearing summary memo**

Produce one document that captures all of the above:
- Hearing date, location, and attendance
- Summary of testimony received
- Themes identified in the board debrief
- Any adjustments made to the budget
- Final language of the three findings

This memo becomes part of the official record. It is proof that your board ran a complete governance process, not a check-the-box hearing.

**This closes the engagement loop**

The full arc: Fall listening sessions → January goals → February crosswalk → March alignment → April hearing → finalized findings → adoption vote.

Every step built on the one before it. The hearing is where the community gets to ask: "Did you actually listen in the fall?" Your findings answer that question.
