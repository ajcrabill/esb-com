<h2>Budget Alignment: 4-Question Audit Checklist</h2>

<p class="checklist-intro">
  Use this checklist to assess how well your school board's budget aligns with the student outcome goals you've adopted. For each question, check the box that best describes where your board is today. This is a self-assessment tool — no email or registration required. Print it, bring it to your next board meeting, and start the conversation.
</p>

<p><strong>Date:</strong> ____________________ &emsp; <strong>Board:</strong> ____________________</p>

<hr/>

<div class="checklist-item">
  <h3><span class="checklist-checkbox"></span> Q1: Goal Recall</h3>
  <p class="checklist-question">
    <strong>Can every board member name the district's top 3 student outcome goals from memory?</strong>
  </p>
  <p class="checklist-note">
    If goals aren't top-of-mind for every member, the budget can't be aligned to them. A board that can't recite its goals hasn't internalized them — and that absence shows up in every budget vote.
  </p>
  <p>
    <label><input type="checkbox" disabled/> Yes — every member can name all 3 from memory</label><br/>
    <label><input type="checkbox" disabled/> Partially — most members can, but not consistently</label><br/>
    <label><input type="checkbox" disabled/> No — goals aren't memorized or referenced regularly</label>
  </p>
</div>

<div class="checklist-item">
  <h3><span class="checklist-checkbox"></span> Q2: Line-Item Scrutiny</h3>
  <p class="checklist-question">
    <strong>Can you look at any line item in the budget and say whether it serves a goal?</strong>
  </p>
  <p class="checklist-note">
    Every dollar of public money the board approves should trace back to a student outcome goal. If a line item can't be linked to a goal, it's a sign of mission drift — or worse, spending that actively undermines board priorities.
  </p>
  <p>
    <label><input type="checkbox" disabled/> Yes — every line item maps to a specific goal</label><br/>
    <label><input type="checkbox" disabled/> Sometimes — major categories are linked, but smaller items aren't</label><br/>
    <label><input type="checkbox" disabled/> No — the budget isn't organized around goals at all</label>
  </p>
</div>

<div class="checklist-item">
  <h3><span class="checklist-checkbox"></span> Q3: Budget Metrics in Progress Monitoring</h3>
  <p class="checklist-question">
    <strong>Does your board's progress monitoring include a budget metric?</strong>
  </p>
  <p class="checklist-note">
    Progress monitoring is how a board holds itself (and the superintendent) accountable. If no metric tracks whether the budget is actually funding what the board says matters most, then the monitoring system has a blind spot — and the community has no way to see if the board's priorities match its spending.
  </p>
  <p>
    <label><input type="checkbox" disabled/> Yes — budget alignment is a standing metric in progress reports</label><br/>
    <label><input type="checkbox" disabled/> Partially — budget comes up in monitoring but isn't a formal metric</label><br/>
    <label><input type="checkbox" disabled/> No — there is no budget-related metric in board monitoring</label>
  </p>
</div>

<div class="checklist-item">
  <h3><span class="checklist-checkbox"></span> Q4: Priority Framework for Tough Choices</h3>
  <p class="checklist-question">
    <strong>Has your board agreed on a priority framework for making tough budget trade-off decisions?</strong>
  </p>
  <p class="checklist-note">
    When budget cuts are needed — and they will be — a pre-agreed framework prevents last-minute politics from driving decisions. Boards with a framework decide on principle. Boards without one decide under pressure, often inconsistently.
  </p>
  <p>
    <label><input type="checkbox" disabled/> Yes — the board has adopted a written priority framework</label><br/>
    <label><input type="checkbox" disabled/> In development — the board is working on one</label><br/>
    <label><input type="checkbox" disabled/> No — no framework exists or has been discussed</label>
  </p>
</div>

<hr/>

<h3>Scoring</h3>
<p>
  <strong>4 "Yes" checks:</strong> Your board has strong budget-to-goal alignment. Keep monitoring.<br/>
  <strong>3 "Yes" checks:</strong> Solid foundation. Close the remaining gap with focused attention.<br/>
  <strong>2 "Yes" checks:</strong> Misalignment is likely costing student outcomes. Start with the weakest area.<br/>
  <strong>1 or 0 "Yes" checks:</strong> Your board's budget is disconnected from its goals. This is the single highest-leverage improvement available.
</p>

<br/>

<h3>Next Steps</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Score of 3-4:</strong> Share this checklist with your superintendent and use it as a framework for the next budget discussion.</li>
  <li><strong>Score of 1-2:</strong> Start with Q1 — get every board member to memorize the goals. Then move to Q4 to establish a priority framework. The line-item audit (Q2) and budget metric (Q3) follow naturally.</li>
  <li><strong>Score of 0:</strong> Go back to the fundamentals. <a href="/framework/">Review the Effective School Boards framework</a> and start with adopting SMART student outcome goals.</li>
</ul>
