<h1>Election Governance Insights</h1>
<p class="lead">A three-part video series from AJ Crabill on what the research says about board turnover, superintendent departure risk, and how to election-proof your school board.</p>

<hr>

<div class="video-card my-4">
  <h3><a href="/governance/election-insights/43-percent-turnover">The 43% Turnover Story — What It Means for Your School Board</a></h3>
  <p>Nearly half of all school board seats turn over every election. AJ Crabill breaks down the Ihrke &amp; Ford research and the Cy-Fair ISD case study to explain why institutional memory evaporates — and what boards can do about it.</p>
  <p><strong>Sources:</strong> Ihrke &amp; Ford (UW-Milwaukee), Cy-Fair ISD Board Election History</p>
</div>

<div class="video-card my-4">
  <h3><a href="/governance/election-insights/superintendent-departure-risk">When Your Board Flips — Superintendent Departure Risk Climbs 67%</a></h3>
  <p>Sixty-seven percent. That's the probability a superintendent will leave within one year of the next board election. Based on Grissom's research at Vanderbilt and RAND Corporation data on the superintendent pipeline.</p>
  <p><strong>Sources:</strong> Grissom et al. (Vanderbilt), RAND Corporation, AASA Superintendent Survey</p>
</div>

<div class="video-card my-4">
  <h3><a href="/governance/election-insights/election-proof-your-board">How to Election-Proof Your Board — 3 Strategies</a></h3>
  <p>Staggered terms, governance onboarding, and board-superintendent succession planning — three structural changes that keep governance trajectory intact through election season, no legislation required.</p>
  <p><strong>Sources:</strong> EdWeek Research Center, ESB Governance Best Practices Framework, Grissom (Vanderbilt)</p>
